Fennec Fox

Its name comes from the species' Arabic name: fanak

The fennec fox is a small crepuscular fox native to the deserts of North Africa, they can be found in territories such as:

  1. Western Sahara
  2. Sinai Peninsula
  3. Arabia

Its most distinctive feature is its unusually large ears, wich serve to dissipate heat and listen for underground prey.
Its coat, ears and kidney fuctions have adapted to de desert environment with high temperatures and little water.

Here are some characteristics of the specie:

  • Family: Canidae
  • Type: Mammals
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Weight: 2.2 to 3.3 pounds
  • Size: 9.5 to 16 inches; tail: 7 to 12.2 inches

The fennec has a life span of up to 14 years in captivity and about 10 years in the wild.
Its main predators are the eagle owl, jackals and other large mammals. Fennec families dig out burrows in the sand
for habitation and protection.

In the wild, fennec foxes eat insects —particularly grasshoppers and locusts— as well as small rodents, lizards, birds and their eggs.
They will also consume roots, fruits and leaves,which help them to hydrate.

The fennec's fur is prized by the indigenous peoples of North Africa, and in some parts of the world, it is considered an exotic pet.


The fennec is the smallest species of fox.
